Confidence Index: Design Insights
The concept is sound and makes the output much more defensible. Here's how I'd approach it and where to be careful:
Two-tier application is more powerful than one:
Rather than a single filter at the end, apply the Confidence Index at two points in the pipeline:
Tier 1 — Source Filter (Phase 1.5): Score each Perplexity citation URL before it gets uploaded to NotebookLM. Only pass URLs that clear 80%. This keeps NotebookLM from being grounded in low-quality sources to begin with.
Tier 2 — Claim Filter (Phase 5 synthesis): For each extracted claim/finding, Claude scores it inline and tags it. Claims below 80% are demoted to an "Unverified / Low Confidence" appendix rather than discarded — they're preserved for your review but excluded from the main synthesis.

Weight

One honest constraint to flag:
Claude cannot perform real-time fact-checking against ground truth. "Accuracy" in the index is therefore a proxy — it measures corroboration and source quality, not verified factual correctness. The index should be described as a confidence in source quality and claim consensus, not a claim-truth score. This distinction matters for how you communicate results.
Currency nuance:
For fast-moving topics (AI, biotech, geopolitics), a 2022 peer-reviewed paper may score lower on currency than a 2025 conference preprint — even though the preprint has lower traditional credibility. I'd recommend making the currency window topic-aware: the command should ask Claude to estimate the topic's half-life before scoring, then apply the currency window accordingly.

MEDIUM — Degrades quality or UX
4. No extraction length validation (Phase 4)

5. Context window pressure in Phase 5
Both raw texts are loaded into the conversation for synthesis. If each platform produces 5,000+ words, that's 10,000+ words of source material plus the entire command prompt. For deep topics, this could hit limits. Recommend instructing Claude to summarize each raw text into ~2,000 words of key claims before scoring — or to process claims in batches if the raw texts exceed a threshold (e.g., 8,000 words combined).

Each poll round requires the user to type "yes". Over two platforms (Perplexity + NotebookLM), that could be 20-40 manual confirmations across 15-30 minutes. When enumerating claims across both platforms, the same finding will often appear in both. Without explicit dedup instructions, Claude might score the same claim twice — once as a Perplexity claim and once as a NotebookLM claim. The scoring step should instruct Claude to merge identical or near-identical claims and attribute them to (Both), then score once.
LOW — Polish and edge cases
